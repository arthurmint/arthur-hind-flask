Kirchoff's Laws

Kirchoff's Current Law (KCL)

Kirchoff's current law states that the total current entering a node is equal to the total current leaving that node, or the net current flowing into node equals zero.

Consider the circuit below. To find an expression for I1 in terms of I2 and I3, we can apply Kirchoff's current law at the bottom node.

$I_3 = I_4$ since they are not separated by any nodes and the current flowing into a resistor equals that flowing out. Therefore, entering the bottom node we have I1 and I4 and leaving this node, we have I2. Thus we can write: $$I_1 + I_4 = I_2$$ $$I_1 + I_3 = I_2$$ $$I_1 = I_2 - I_3$$



Kirchoff's Voltage Law (KVL)

Consider every complete loop in a circuit, KVL states that the net sum of the potential across all the devices in every complete loop equals zero.
